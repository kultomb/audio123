# DIALOGUE TRANSLATION RULES

## 1. WORD-FOR-MEANING ACCURACY (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
- Translate the EXACT meaning of each line. Do not substitute words with similar-but-different concepts.
- If a line says "thay pin" → you say "thay pin". NOT "đổi pin", NOT "sửa pin", NOT "tháo pin".
- If a line says "sửa điện thoại" → you say "sửa điện thoại". NOT "tháo điện thoại", NOT "sửa máy".
- **WRONG examples that VIOLATE this rule:**
  - Source: "thay pin" → WRONG output: "đổi cửa hàng" (completely different meaning!)
  - Source: "sửa điện thoại" → WRONG output: "tháo điện thoại" (different action!)
  - Source: "thay màn hình" → WRONG output: "sửa màn hình" (different action!)
- **ALWAYS cross-check**: After translating, ask yourself "Does this line mean exactly what the original said?"

## 2. CHARACTER VOICE & EMOTION
- Each character speaks differently. Listen to HOW they say things, not just WHAT they say.
- **Flirty/friendly girl customer**: playful tone, drawn-out words, "anh ơi", "ghê", "thật á", giggly energy
- **Calm male technician**: short replies, matter-of-fact, "ừ", "được", "đúng rồi"
- **Tech muttering to himself**: frustrated, short bursts, "sao lại...", "vẫn còn... mà"
- Match emotional intensity: excitement → lively, frustration → clipped, casual → relaxed

## 3. NATURAL SPOKEN VIETNAMESE
- Sound like a REAL Vietnamese person in a REAL conversation. Not a textbook. Not a news broadcast.
- **Sentence particles are MANDATORY for natural speech**: "nhé", "nha", "rồi", "luôn", "đấy", "thôi", "nhỉ", "hả", "ạ", "đi", "mà", "cơ", "chứ", "vậy", "nè"
- **Contractions**: "không" → "ko"/"hông" (casual), "được không" → "được ko"
- **Interjections**: "Ừ", "À", "Ơ", "Này", "Thôi", "Ối", "Trời ơi", "Ôi"
- **Hesitations**: "ừm...", "ờ..." — only if present in original

## 4. PRONOUNS — CRITICAL FOR CHARACTER IDENTITY
- Customer (young woman) to male tech: "em" (self), "anh" (him)
- Male tech to young woman customer: "anh" (self), "em" (her)
- Tech thinking to himself: "mình" or "tôi"
- NEVER mix up who is speaking to whom. The pronouns tell the listener who's talking.

## 5. TIMING & BREVITY FOR DUBBING
- This will be read by TTS. Must fit within the timestamp duration.
- Under 2 sec → 1-5 words. 2-4 sec → one short sentence. 4+ sec → max one full sentence.
- Single-word reactions stay single-word: "Ừ.", "Được.", "Sao?", "Thật á?"
- Cut filler words but KEEP the core meaning and emotional tone.

## 6. SELF-CONTAINED BLOCKS
- Each block is voiced independently. Make each one speakable as a standalone line.
- If original splits a sentence across blocks, let each fragment be natural on its own.
- **NO "..." bridging between blocks.** End with period, comma, or natural particle.
