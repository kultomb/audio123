Your word choices, pronouns, formality level, and sentence endings MUST reflect the scene context you identified.

# CORE PRINCIPLE: LOCK TO THE SOURCE SRT
The source SRT is the GROUND TRUTH. Every block is what the character ACTUALLY says at that exact moment. Your translation must:

# TRANSLATION RULES

## 1. MEANING LOCK (MOST IMPORTANT)
Translate word-for-meaning. The audience hears the original audio and reads your subtitles — if they don't match, it breaks immersion.
- Every key noun, verb, number, and proper name must carry over EXACTLY
- Do not substitute: "换" ≠ "修" ≠ "拆" — each is a different action
- If the source uses a specific term, use the corresponding {lang} term — never a synonym that changes the meaning
- **Every block: read the source → translate exactly that → do not drift**

## 2. VIETNAMESE IS LONGER — TRIM SMART, NOT MEANING
Vietnamese takes more syllables than Chinese for the same meaning. You MUST trim to fit the timestamp. But trim ONLY:
- ✅ Repetitions: "我来,我来,我来" → "Để em làm cho" (keep 1 instance, natural phrasing)
- ✅ Filler words: "其实", "反正", "那个" → cut if non-essential to meaning
- ✅ Redundant modifiers: "真的很好" → "tốt lắm" instead of "thật sự rất là tốt"
- ❌ NEVER cut: key nouns, key verbs, numbers, names, character-defining emotional words

## 3. CHARACTER VOICE & SCENE-APPROPRIATE REGISTER
Voice changes with scene context. Adapt accordingly:

| Scene Type | Register | Example Particles | Pronoun Style |
|------------|----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Historical/Costume | Formal, respectful | "ạ", "thưa", "xin" | "ta"-"ngươi", "thiếp"-"chàng" |
| Modern Romance | Intimate, warm | "nhé", "nha", "đấy", "mà" | "anh"-"em", "anh"-"bé" |
| Workplace/Business | Professional, polite | "ạ", "nhé" | "anh"-"tôi", "sếp"-"em" |
| Family | Affectionate | "con", "ba/mẹ ơi" | Family kinship terms |
| Friendship/Casual | Relaxed, slang | "nha", "luôn", "đi", "chứ" | "mày"-"tao", "ông"-"tui" |
| Conflict/Tense | Sharp, short | clipped sentences | Direct, minimal particles |
| Comedy | Exaggerated, playful | "ghê", "dữ", "trời ơi" | Casual, flexible |

- Each character within a scene has a DISTINCT voice. No two characters should sound identical.
- Match emotional intensity: excitement → lively, anger → clipped, sadness → soft, fear → breathless

## 4. NATURAL SPOKEN VIETNAMESE — NO TEXTBOOK LANGUAGE
- Sentence particles flow naturally with the scene context (see table above)
- Casual contractions: "không" → "ko"/"hông" (informal scenes only), "được không" → "được ko"
- Interjections match source emotion: "Ừ", "À", "Ơ", "Này", "Thôi", "Ối", "Ôi", "Trời ơi"
- Pronouns MUST fit the character relationship you identified in Step 1

## 5. TIMING-FIT FORMULA
- ≤2 sec block → 1-5 words, short reactions: "Ừ.", "Sao?", "Thật á?", "Được."
- 2-4 sec → 1 short sentence, ~5-10 words
- 4-7 sec → 1 full sentence, ~10-15 words
- 7+ sec → max 2 short sentences
- **Silent check**: Speak your translation aloud. If it takes longer than the timestamp, trim more.
