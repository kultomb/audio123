# DIALOGUE STYLE RULES

## 1. CHARACTER VOICE PRESERVATION (CRITICAL)
- Each character has a unique way of speaking. Preserve their personality:
  - **Formal/authoritative character** → formal register in {lang}
  - **Casual/young character** → slang, contractions, informal particles
  - **Angry/emotional character** → sharp, short sentences; exclamations
  - **Sad/reflective character** → softer tone, longer pauses implied
- Match the emotional intensity of each line exactly.
- **Dialogue must stick close to each character's exact words** — do not paraphrase or summarize what they say. Each line should reflect what that specific character said, in their voice.

## 2. DUBBING-SAFE PACING & CONCISENESS
The translated text will be used for TTS voiceover. If the translation is too long, the audio will play too fast.
- **Film dialogue is timed to actor delivery.** Every block's duration must be respected.
- Short blocks (under 2 seconds) → extremely short phrases, often 1-3 words.
- Medium blocks (2-4 seconds) → one short sentence.
- Long blocks (4+ seconds) → one full sentence max.
- In dialogue, blocks often contain single words or short reactions ("Yeah.", "What?", "No way."). Translate these as equivalently short.
- Remove filler words and redundant modifiers while keeping the character's intent.

## 3. NATURAL CONVERSATION FLOW
- Use real spoken language: contractions, interruptions, overlapping-thought indicators.
- Translate interjections naturally: "Ừ", "À", "Ơ", "Này", "Thôi", "Thật á?".
- Pauses and hesitations: "ừm...", "ờ..." (only if in original).
- Question tags: "đúng không?", "phải không?", "nhỉ?", "hả?".
- The conversation must flow naturally across blocks — like real back-and-forth between characters.

## 4. SELF-CONTAINED BLOCKS - BUT CONVERSATION-AWARE
- Each block is voiced separately. Make each one speakable alone.
- However, the conversation should feel connected across blocks.
- **NO ELLIPSIS BRIDGING**: Do NOT use `...` to connect blocks. End with natural punctuation.
- If a sentence is split across blocks, let each fragment stand naturally — just like people speak in phrases.

# VIETNAMESE-SPECIFIC LOCALIZATION (when {lang} is Vietnamese)

## TONE & REGISTER
- **Casual, conversational Vietnamese**. Sound like a real person talking, not a textbook or news anchor.
- Match the character: shop owner, customer, technician, friend — each has their own speech style.
- **Sentence Endings**: Use natural particles — "nhé", "nha", "rồi", "luôn", "đấy", "thôi", "nhỉ", "hả", "ạ", "đi", "mà", "cơ", "chứ". Avoid stiff endings.
- **Contractions & Casual Speech**: "không" → "ko"/"hông", "được không" → "được ko", "thế nào" → "thế nào", "làm sao" → "làm sao". Use naturally, don't force.

## PRONOUNS BY CHARACTER CONTEXT
- **Shop/service context** (repair shop, store):
  - Customer to technician: "anh" (male tech), "chị" (female tech), self: "em"
  - Technician to customer: "em" (younger customer), "anh"/"chị" (older), self: "anh"/"chị"/"em"
  - Technician thinking to self: "mình"/"tôi"
- **Friends/peers**: "tôi"/"tớ"/"mình" for self, "bạn"/"cậu"/"ông"/"bà" depending on closeness
- Never use "chúng tôi" (too formal). Use "tụi mình"/"bọn mình" instead.

## TECH/REPAIR CONTEXT (Phone repair, electronics)
- KEEP original English acronyms in SRT text: `MOSFET`, `VBAT`, `VPH`, `VBUS`, `CPU`, `UFS`, `IC`, `I2C`, `VDD`, `VDDQ`, `APT`, `eMMC`, `NAND`, `mAh`
- "bị lỗi" not "có lỗi", "bị chạm" not "có chạm", "bị đứt" not "có đứt"
- "mình kiểm tra" not "tôi sẽ kiểm tra", "xem thế nào" not "xem như thế nào"
- "xong rồi" not "đã hoàn thành", "chưa được" not "không thành công"
- "thay pin", "thay màn hình", "sửa điện thoại", "sạc", "cáp", "bảo hành"
- "dung lượng", "pin rơi", "không bật máy được"

## COMMON SPOKEN PATTERNS
- "Đây, anh thấy..." / "Này, em xem..." (Here, look...)
- "Giờ mình..." (Now I'll...)
- "Thế là..." (So...)
- "Xong!" / "Được rồi!" (Done! / Alright!)
- "Để em..." / "Để anh..." (Let me...)
- "Cảm ơn anh/em nhé." (Thank you!)
- "Tạm biệt anh/em." (Goodbye!)

## FORBIDDEN PATTERNS (sound unnatural in spoken Vietnamese dialogue)
- "Tôi sẽ cho bạn xem" → "Để anh cho em xem"
- "Bạn có thể thấy" → "Em thấy đấy"
- "Điều đó có nghĩa là" → "Tức là" / "Nghĩa là"
- "Như các bạn đã biết" → skip entirely, just state the fact
- "Chúng ta hãy" → "Mình cùng" / "Giờ mình"
